Types of Bonds

Government Bonds

Government bonds are debt securities issued by the government to support government spending and obligations. These are considered low-risk investments as they are backed by the government's credit.

Corporate Bonds

Corporate bonds are issued by companies to raise capital for various purposes such as expanding operations, mergers, or acquisitions. These bonds typically offer higher interest rates compared to government bonds but come with higher risk.

Municipal Bonds

Municipal bonds are issued by states, cities, or other governmental entities to fund public projects like schools, highways, or infrastructure. Interest earned on municipal bonds is often tax-exempt, making them attractive to investors.

Convertible Bonds

Convertible bonds are corporate bonds that can be converted into a predetermined number of the issuing company's equity shares. They offer the benefit of fixed interest payments and potential appreciation of the underlying stock.

Junk Bonds

Junk bonds, also known as high-yield bonds, are bonds that are rated below investment grade. They offer higher yields to compensate for the higher risk of default. These bonds are issued by companies with weaker financial positions.

Sovereign Bonds

Sovereign bonds are issued by a national government and denominated in a foreign currency. These bonds are used to raise capital in international markets and are subject to currency exchange risk.

Agency Bonds

Agency bonds are issued by government-sponsored enterprises (GSEs) or federal agencies. These bonds are typically used to support public policy objectives such as affordable housing and agriculture.

Foreign Bonds

Foreign bonds are issued in a domestic market by a foreign entity in the domestic market's currency. They are subject to the regulatory and market conditions of the domestic market.

Green Bonds

Green bonds are issued to fund projects that have positive environmental or climate benefits. These bonds support investments in renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean transportation, and sustainable water management.
