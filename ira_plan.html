IRA Retirement Plan Information

What is an IRA Plan?

An Individual Retirement Account (IRA) is a savings account with tax advantages that individuals can use to invest for retirement.

Comparison of Different IRA Plans

Plan Type Contribution Limit (2024) Eligibility Tax Treatment
Traditional IRA $6,000 (Under 50), $7,000 (50 and over) Anyone with earned income Contributions may be tax-deductible; earnings grow tax-deferred
Roth IRA $6,000 (Under 50), $7,000 (50 and over) Subject to income limits Contributions are not tax-deductible; qualified withdrawals are tax-free
Simplified Employee Pension (SEP) IRA No specific limit; employer contributions Self-employed individuals and small business owners Employer contributions are tax-deductible; earnings grow tax-deferred
Self-Employed 401(k) (Solo 401(k)) $58,000 (Under 50), $64,500 (50 and over) Self-employed individuals with no employees other than a spouse Contributions may be tax-deductible; earnings grow tax-deferred
