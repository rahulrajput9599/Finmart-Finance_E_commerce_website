Investment Management

Investment Details

Investment management involves the professional management of various securities and assets to meet specified investment goals for the benefit of investors. It encompasses a range of activities, including asset allocation, portfolio construction, and risk management.

There are various types of investment management options available, including mutual funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), hedge funds, and robo-advisors. Each option has its own set of characteristics, advantages, and disadvantages.

Comparison

Investment Option Advantages Disadvantages
Mutual Funds Diversification, Professional Management Management Fees, Potential Underperformance
ETFs Low Cost, Liquidity Trading Fees, Tracking Error
Hedge Funds Potential for High Returns High Fees, Limited Access
Robo-Advisors Low Cost, Automated Portfolio Management Limited Customization, Lack of Human Touch
