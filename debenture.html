What are Debentures?

Debentures are a type of debt instrument that are not secured by physical assets or collateral. They are backed only by the general creditworthiness and reputation of the issuer. Debentures are usually issued by large corporations and governments to raise funds for various purposes.

Types of Debentures

  • Convertible Debentures: These can be converted into equity shares of the issuing company after a certain period.
  • Non-Convertible Debentures: These cannot be converted into equity shares and usually carry a higher rate of interest.
  • Secured Debentures: These are backed by a charge on the assets of the company, providing greater security to investors.
  • Unsecured Debentures: These are not backed by any assets and carry a higher risk compared to secured debentures.
